Daily Caffeine

Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 18, 2026

Summary

Daily Caffeine is designed to run locally on your Mac. It does not require an account, does not include analytics, does not track you, and does not send your schedule, selected apps, selected folders, or workspace activity to a remote service.

Information Stored On Your Mac

The app may store settings such as your daily schedule, active weekdays, break window, selected duration preferences, selected coding apps, selected workspace folders, notification preferences, and start-at-login preference. These settings are stored locally for the app to function.

Workspace Activity

If you enable Workspace Activity, Daily Caffeine watches folders that you explicitly select. It uses that local activity signal to decide whether to keep your Mac awake while a selected app, such as Codex or Claude, is active. The app does not upload file contents, file names, project paths, or workspace activity to a server.

Notifications

Daily Caffeine may send local macOS notifications when a keep-awake session starts or ends. Notifications may mention whether the session was triggered by schedule, manual override, or workspace activity.

Data Sharing

Daily Caffeine does not sell personal data, does not share personal data with advertisers, and does not include third-party tracking SDKs.

Contact

For support or privacy questions, use the support page: Daily Caffeine Support.
